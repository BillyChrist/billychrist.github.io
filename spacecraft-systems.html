Spacecraft Systems Design

Large-Aperture Telescope to GEO

Space mission design project delivering a 1 km class large-aperture telescope to GEO, with focus on systems engineering, risk, cost, and thermal design.

Mission Overview

  • End-to-end mission concept for deploying a large aperture telescope to Geostationary Orbit (GEO).
  • Analyzed subsystem interactions including propulsion, ACS, power, C&DH, thermal, and telecom.
  • Developed mission block diagrams and system connectivity to promote fault tolerance.

Risk Analysis

  • Identified pre-mitigation risks across subsystems (mechanical, GN&C, C&DH, power, thermal, ACS, propulsion, telecom, payload, full system).
  • Developed mitigation strategies such as redundancy, safe-mode protocols, sensor fusion, and standardized communication protocols.
  • Summarized risk matrices for pre- and post-mitigation states.

Cost Analysis

  • Estimated subsystem costs and life-cycle phase budgets.
  • Characterized major cost drivers including launch, assembly & integration, and early-phase design.
  • Summarized total mission costs with and without launch vehicle.

Thermal Design

  • Sized radiators, heaters, and thermal coatings to maintain key components within allowable temperature limits for LEO and GEO.
  • Evaluated passive vs. hybrid thermal architectures and recommended redundant passive systems.
  • Performed orbital position thermal analysis and radiator sizing (e.g., ~2.4 m² total radiator area).
