Spacecraft Systems Design

Kilometer-Class Space Telescope Mission Design

Space mission design study exploring the construction of a 1 km-class space telescope assembled in orbit. The project focused on the systems engineering required to transport and assemble the observatory, including launch architecture, orbital transfer vehicle design, subsystem trade studies, and mission-level cost and risk analysis.

My primary contributions focused on system-level architecture and mission feasibility analysis. I developed the spacecraft system block diagram and high-level subsystem layout, performed mission risk assessment and cost analysis, and conducted a detailed thermal system design study for the orbital transfer vehicle and telescope infrastructure. These analyses helped establish the overall spacecraft configuration and operational constraints used throughout the mission design study.

Mission Overview

This project explored the feasibility of constructing a kilometer-scale space telescope in orbit using modular components launched from Earth and assembled near the L2 point. The concept expands on the architecture of the James Webb Space Telescope by proposing a significantly larger segmented aperture that would be constructed in space from multiple launched structural and optical elements.

Our team was responsible for designing the orbital transfer vehicle (OTV) and logistics architecture required to transport telescope components from Earth orbit to the L2 assembly site. The study evaluated launch strategies, transportation infrastructure, and spacecraft systems needed to support a multi-launch construction campaign for a large-scale space observatory.

The mission analysis included launch vehicle selection, payload mass budgeting, trajectory planning, and spacecraft subsystem design. A dedicated OTV platform was developed to deliver structural elements, optical segments, and supporting hardware from launch vehicles operating in Earth orbit to the L2 construction orbit where the telescope would be assembled.

  • Developed mission architecture for in-space construction of a kilometer-scale telescope near the Sun–Earth L2 point.

  • Designed an orbital transfer vehicle (OTV) responsible for transporting telescope components from Earth orbit to the L2 assembly site.

  • Evaluated launch vehicle options and developed a multi-launch campaign using commercially available heavy-lift vehicles.

  • Performed payload mass budgeting and logistics analysis for transporting structural and optical components.

  • Conducted subsystem trade studies for propulsion, attitude control, communications, power, thermal, and command & data handling.

  • Analyzed mission cost, development timelines, and risk factors associated with large-scale orbital construction.
